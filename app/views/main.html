Providing Software Development Management for Non-Trival Projects

When non-technical managers are assigned to software projects, they often describe their experience akin to herding cats.

You want to hire the best people. They need technical expertise, leadership skills, and the ability to translate technical problems into language that managers of every level can understand.

Projects very rarely have technical problems. Although the issues may be hidden in the language of technology, at the root of most problems are people.

Software Development is about people

Data-driven websites have a different sort of risk than most projects, so when typical managers step into the role of project manager, they're blindsided by sudden changes in scope with technical explanations a mile long. It would cause anyone to panic.

What they forgot is that the real issue, the only important issue, has always been about people.
